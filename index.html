Growing up


Growing up, I was always taught to embrace the beauty of my natural hair! However, due to society and the lack of black hair products available, I was subjected to do things such as straighten my hair to conform to white standards of beauty.

I can't remember exactly what happened, but at age 10, I decided I wanted to embrace my natural hair and discontinue straightening it.
Then, starting at age 12 up until high school, I began washing and maintaining my hair myself. Of couse, others helped along the way, such as my family and very trusted hair stylists.

College: Freshman Year


The tides began to turn when I moved to college. I was 18 and decided to attend a university over 700 miles away from home. Therefore, it became solely up to me to maintain my hair. The task was much easier said than done. On the outside, my hair continued to look good. But, behind closed doors, there were late nights, breakdowns, excess dandrift, breakage, matting, and overall dilapidation.


Locing my hair

Locing
verb
pronounced: lock-ing
n. loc[s]
syn. dreadlocks

a hairstyle, tradiaitonally performed on black hair, where hair strands are coiled, braided, twisted, palm-rolled, or interlocked to create a rope-like appearance.


After many discussions and heart-to-hearts with family members, the internet, and trusted stylists, we all decided that locing my hair would be the best solution for me. At first, I was wary because the process requires commitment. However, in the end, I decided to take a leap of faith and begin the journey of restoring my hair.

I am currently 1 year and 5 months in the locing process and my hair is the healthiest it has ever been! Having healthy, natural hair that is easy to maintain has made me not only look and feel good but has increased my confidence in myself! I feel like I can now take on the world!
